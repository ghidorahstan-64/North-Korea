What To Do in The DMZ?

The Korean Demilitarized Zone (DMZ) is one of the most heavily guarded borders in the world. Visitors can join guided tours that provide insight into the division of North and South Korea, with stops at key observation points and historical landmarks.

Popular attractions include infiltration tunnels dug beneath the border, the Joint Security Area (JSA), and the Freedom Bridge. These sites offer a unique look into military history and the ongoing tensions between the two Koreas.

Visiting the DMZ is both a historical and emotional experience. It serves as a reminder of the Korean War and the continued division of the peninsula, while also representing hope for eventual peace and reunification.
