What To Do in Pyongyang?

Pyongyang, the capital of North Korea, is known for its wide streets, grand architecture, and monuments. The city is carefully planned and features impressive landmarks that showcase the country’s identity and political ideology.

Major attractions include the Juche Tower, which represents self-reliance, and the Arch of Triumph, built to commemorate resistance against Japanese rule. The Pyongyang Metro is also notable for its deep tunnels and elaborately decorated stations.

Visitors to Pyongyang can explore monuments, attend cultural performances, and visit museums. Guided tours often include stops at key landmarks, offering insights into the country’s history, culture, and daily life.
